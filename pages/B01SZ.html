Autonomous Learning Project (ALP)

The following are some ideas you can use in Basic 1:

UNIT 1

Students introduce themselves to each other. With a partner, they must ask/answer questions about their names, nicknames and telephone numbers. They should use greetings in the conversation.

Students can ask and answer questions about a friend or family member‚Äôs personal information and his/her favorites (book, movie, TV show, etc.)

UNIT 2

Students choose a country individually, and in pairs, they ask/answer questions on its capital city and its characteristics. Then, students switch roles.

They can also ask/answer questions about a city they want to visit and include information on where it is, what it is famous for and what it is like (using positive and negative adjectives)..

UNIT 3

Students choose a gift individually, and in pairs, they ask/answer questions on its characteristics.

Students talk about an item that could be a good gift for a friend or family member. They can also ask/answer questions about the party they want to go and include information on where it is, ( using descriptive and demonstratives adjectives)

Autonomous Learning Project Criteria

4 pts -
  • Appropriate use of all or almost all of the target language forms necessary for the task.
  • Comprehensibility is effortless.
  • On Task. Very effective and creative, logically structured all or almost all of the times.
  • All or almost all of the content is adequate and pertinent for the task.
  • Outstanding use of presentation support (intonation, gestures, visuals, videos, etc.) appropriate and pertinent for the task.

3 pts -
  • Appropriate use of many of the target language forms necessary for the task. Comprehensibility is not affected.
  • On Task. Quite effective, logically structured many of the times. - Much of the content is adequate for the task
  • Quite adequate use of presentation support (intonation, gestures, visuals, videos, etc.) appropriate for the task.

2 pts -
  • Appropriate use of barely enough of the target language forms necessary for the task.
  • Comprehensibility is not significantly affected. - On Task. Moderately effective, logically structured at times.
  • Content is moderately adequate for the task. - Moderately adequate use of presentation support (intonation, gestures, visuals, videos, etc.) appropriate for the task.

1 pts -
  • Appropriate use of few of the target language forms necessary for the task. Makes comprehensibility difficult.
  • On task, but ineffective. Disorganized most of the times. - Much of the content is inadequate content for the task.
  • inadequate or insufficient use of presentation support (intonation, gestures, visuals, videos, etc.) Appropriate for the task.

0 pts -
  • Inappropriate use of all or almost all of the target language forms needed for the task.Comprehensibility is significantly affected
  • Off Task. Very ineffective. Continuously unclear and disorganized all the time.
  • All content is inadequate for the task. - No use of presentation support (intonation, gestures, visuals, videos, etc.) appropriate for the task.
